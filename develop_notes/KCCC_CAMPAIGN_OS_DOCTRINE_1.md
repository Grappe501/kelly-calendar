## Doctrine #1

> **The operator defines reality. The system interprets reality.**

Everything else follows from that.

### Operator responsibilities (define reality)

* Who to meet  
* When to meet  
* Priorities  
* Political judgment  
* Relationship strategy  
* Final schedule  

### System responsibilities (interpret reality)

* Detect conflicts  
* Find opportunities  
* Identify missing preparation  
* Warn about travel issues  
* Surface relationship context  
* Suggest improvements  
* Simulate outcomes  

The system **never silently changes the operator’s intent**.

---

## Why this matters

Campaign context often outweighs efficiency:

* A lower-priority event may still be politically essential  
* A difficult trip may be worth making  
* A conflict may be intentional  
* A relationship may outweigh optimization  

The system informs; campaign leadership retains authority.

---

## Module test (every future feature)

> **Is this defining reality, or interpreting reality?**

| Answer | Layer |
|--------|--------|
| Defining reality | **Operational** |
| Interpreting reality | **Intelligence** |

Applies to calendar, communications, relationships, volunteers, fundraising, and mission planning alike.

---

## Intelligence presentation pattern (when AI / recommendations ship)

```text
Observation
The event overlaps with travel time.

Reason
The drive exceeds the available buffer by 35 minutes.

Recommendation
Move the meeting to 2:30 PM or depart 30 minutes earlier.

Impact
Removes travel conflict and preserves preparation time.

Action
[Accept]   [Modify]   [Dismiss]
```

Observe → explain why → recommend → operator decides.
